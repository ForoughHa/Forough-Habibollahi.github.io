Forough Habibollahi

Post-doctoral Research Fellow/Data Scientist at Cortical Labs, Melbourne, Australia

Currently, I am a full-time data scientist and computational neuroscience postdoctoral research fellow at Cortical Labs. We are a start-up company that has created DishBrain, which fuses living brain cells onto computing devices to create machines with biological intelligence. Our in-vitro neuronal cultures can learn to play the game of 'Pong'!

Prior to this, I was a Ph.D. student at the Neural Dynamics Laboratory, Department of Biomedical Engineering, Melbourne Brain Center, University of Melbourne, Australia. I studied Calcium Imaging Recordings from freely behaving mice recorded using Miniscopes to investigate the emergence of criticality in hippocampal neural ensembles during cognitive tasks compared to the resting state.

Education & Research Experience

  • École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL): Undertook M.Sc. courses at the School of Life Sciences while working under the supervision of Prof. Johannes Graff and Dr. Bianca Ambrogina Silva at the Brain Mind Institute (BMI).

  • Sharif University of Technology: Earned my B.Sc. degree in Electrical Engineering. As an undergraduate, I worked with Prof. Subburaj Karupppasamy in the Medical Engineering and Design Laboratory at SUTD-MIT, Singapore University of Technology and Design.

  • Collaborated remotely with Prof. Alex Fornito and Dr. Nigel Rogasch at the Turner Institute for Brain and Mental Health, Monash University, to develop a MATLAB toolbox for serial communication with transcranial magnetic stimulation devices.
