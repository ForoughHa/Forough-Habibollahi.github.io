Forough Habibollahi

Data Scientist/Post-doctoral Research Fellow at Cortical Labs, Melbourne, Australia

I am a data scientist and computational neuroscience postdoctoral research fellow at Cortical Labs. Our work focuses on creating biological intelligence by fusing living brain cells with computing devices.

Currently, I am a full-time data scientist and computational neuroscience postdoctoral research fellow at Cortical Labs. We are a start-up company that has created DishBrain, which fuses living brain cells onto computing devices to create machines with biological intelligence. Our in-vitro neuronal cultures can learn to play the game of 'Pong'!

Education & Research Experience

The University of Melbourne

I was a Ph.D. student at the Neural Dynamics Laboratory, Department of Biomedical Engineering. I studied Calcium Imaging Recordings from freely behaving mice using Miniscopes to investigate hippocampal criticality.

EPFL (École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne)

I undertook M.Sc. courses at the School of Life Sciences while working with Prof. Johannes Graff and Dr. Bianca Ambrogina Silva at the Brain Mind Institute (BMI).

SUTD-MIT (Singapore University of Technology and Design)

As a research intern at the Medical Engineering and Design Laboratory, I focused on image processing and analysis of bone CT data for Osteoporosis patients using real data from FBP and SIR methods.

Sharif University of Technology

I earned my B.Sc. degree in Electrical Engineering and worked on the Chaotic Analysis of Neural Activity in the Primary Visual Cortex of an awake monkey.

Monash University (Remote Internship)

As a remote intern at the Turner Institute for Brain and Mental Health, I developed a MATLAB toolbox for serial communication with transcranial magnetic stimulation devices.
